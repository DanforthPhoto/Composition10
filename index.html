This page is for my Top 10 composition constructs.

Rule of Thirds

The concept of rule of thirds involves setting up a shot so that key elements in the picture on on the vertical and horizontal lines that would be created if the shot were split into 9 parts.

Background/Negative Space

In photography, empty/repetitively patterned backgrounds often create a more favourable composition and draw the eye of the viewer to the subject.

Balancing Elements

Balancing elements refer to elements in the picture that keep a shot from being too weighted in any direction.

Leading Lines

The idea of leading lines centers around shots that have strong lines (such as paths, rows of fences) that draw the eye and cause the viewer to follow them while observing the image.

Symmetry and Patterns

Symmetry is not always important in a shot and many pictures benefit from non-symmetry, but a composition that has good symmetry is often more appealing and/or satisfying.

Viewpoint

Changing the viewpoint of a shot or presenting an image from an unusual angle (from the top of a building/from underwater) creates a more interesting composition, even with boring subjects.

Depth

Depth is a very important thing to be considering when taking a picture, be it a macro shot, a landscape, or a portrait. Depth allows a picture to be more real by conveying 3-dimensionality.

Framing

Framing refers to the use of natural or man made frames (windows, gaps in branches) to isolate the subject inside an area.

Cropping

Choosing the area for a shot and “cropping” the shot can let a subject that would otherwise not stand out be more apparent.

Experimentation

Experimentation is exactly what it sounds like - trying out new concepts or inventing your own shot, and sometimes ending up with a completely unique picture.
